## Abstract
The computing industry and the associated academic community are both demanding greater coverage of cybersecurity than is currently delivered by undergraduate computing programs. In this dissertation, I studied project-based capstone courses as one contribution toward closing that gap. Because capstone courses are generally taken in the senior year, those designing instructional experiences can expect students to be grounded in computing fundamentals. As an active-learning context, capstone courses offer students the opportunity to immediately apply what they learn in realistic projects. Through an intervention study, an interview study, and a review of US computer science programs, I found that embedding cybersecurity learning in capstone courses is an effective way to teach foundational sociotechnical principles of cybersecurity. Although a majority of computer science programs require a senior capstone course, most do not explicitly include a cybersecurity component. Accordingly, I assert that including cybersecurity instruction in capstone courses is a valuable contribution to the overall effort to improve cybersecurity learning for undergraduate computing students.
